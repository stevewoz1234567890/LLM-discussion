# Stanford 2026 AI Index (high-level summary)

Stanford HAI published a **423-page** [**2026 AI Index Report**](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report) ([PDF](https://hai.stanford.edu/assets/files/ai_index_report_2026.pdf)).

### Themes (as summarized)

- **Capability vs benchmarks:** Model progress is **accelerating**; leading systems are **matching human performance** on demanding tasks in **coding, science, and math**, which makes **older benchmarks less informative**.
- **U.S. vs China:** The **gap is relatively small** in the big picture. The **U.S. leads** in **top models** and **private investment**; **China leads** on **papers, citations, patents, and deployment**. One headline geographic cut in the report (notable models by area, **2025**, **Epoch AI** data in **Figure 1.1.1**): **United States 50**, **China 30**, with smaller counts elsewhere (e.g. South Korea 5; several regions at 1).
- **Adoption:** Roughly **53% of the global population** reached in **three years**, supporting **business uptake** and **consumer surplus**.
- **Responsible AI:** **Incidents rose** (report cites **362**); **safety reporting** remains **weak**; **tradeoffs** persist.
- **Hardware:** Still **critical**—dependence on **data centers**, **high energy use**, and a **supply chain** where **TSMC** plays an outsized role.
- **Labor:** **Mixed**—**productivity** gains in **support** and **coding** vs **pressure** on some **entry-level software** roles.
- **Medicine:** Among the **most practical near-term** wins: **AI note-taking** and similar tools may **reduce clinician burnout**, though **real-world clinical evidence** is still **thin**.
