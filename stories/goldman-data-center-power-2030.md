# Global data center power demand (Goldman Sachs outlook)

**Goldman Sachs Global Investment Research** expects **global data center electricity demand** to **accelerate**, with the **AI-related share** of that demand rising sharply in projections from **2024** onward (see their stacked breakdown: **US ex-AI**, **US AI**, **RoW ex-AI**, **RoW AI**—historical vs **E**stimated years).

### Headline figures (as reported)

- **+220%** vs **2023** levels, i.e. about **+905 TWh**, to roughly **1,350 TWh** by **2030** (a **record**). Prior forecast was about **+175%**; the revision is tied to **higher AI server shipment** expectations and **more power-intensive AI** server deployment.
- **About 60%** of that **incremental growth** is expected from the **United States** (vs about **50%** in the **prior** estimate). Implied **2030** split: **US ~750 TWh**, **rest of world ~600 TWh**.
- **US data center capacity** projected to rise about **+197%** from **2025** to **2030**, to about **95 GW**.

### Takeaway

The **AI power** component of data center demand is **accelerating** in these projections.
